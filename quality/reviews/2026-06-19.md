## 4. TOP 3 IMPROVEMENTS

**1. Verify topical relevance before generating an opportunity, not just entity relevance**
The analyst treats "government agency + Singapore built environment" as sufficient grounds for an opportunity, without checking whether the underlying signal involves digital twin/BIM/smart building technology. Peck Hay Road is a pure residential GLS tender — nothing in any source connects it to PropTech. Add an explicit filter: "Does this signal directly involve digital twin, BIM, 3D scanning, XR/spatial computing, or smart FM technology? If the connection is inferred rather than stated in the source, discard or flag as speculative — do not generate a submission deadline."

**2. Never invent deadlines, submission channels, or contact instructions not present in the source**
All three opportunities include fabricated procedural details ("submit a proposal by 30 June 2026," "request a meeting by 15 July 2026") that appear nowhere in any source. This is the most actionable-looking part of the report and also the most fabricated. Require the analyst to either quote/cite the actual deadline or contact mechanism found in the source, or explicitly state "no public deadline/contact identified — opportunity requires direct relationship-building."

**3. Surface the strongest real signal instead of the weakest fabricated one**
Lendlease's $40M Product Development Centre and Podium platform, and BCA's Built Environment Transformation Fund (up to 70% funding for digital twin/BIM tooling), are both specific, real, and directly relevant — yet both are reduced to one generic sentence each while the report leads with a fabricated residential-tender opportunity. Reweight opportunity-scoring to prioritize signals with concrete funding mechanisms, named programmes, or platform investments over signals that merely mention "digital transformation" as a buzzword.
